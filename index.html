Define traveler types based on time in country and risk of infection occurring during that period but not becoming symptomatic before arrival. Infection risks are order-of-magnitude risks that may represent different profile types. For instance, someone with a known exposure without personal protective equipment (PPE) would be on the order of 1/10, while a health-care worker treating BDBV patients with PPE would be order 1/100 or 1/1,000, and a traveler with no known exposures would likely be 1/10,000 or less.

Incubation period is assumed to be gamma distributed and is defined, here, by its median and 95th percentile. You can optionally specify an alternate incubation period estimate.

Incubation period

Time from exposure to symptom onset, modeled as a gamma distribution. Estimated from a Bayesian fit (5,000 posterior draws); each value below is the posterior median with a 95% credible interval.

Specify the median and the 95th÷median ratio; the gamma distribution is solved to match.

95th pct ≈ days

Timing and tail-heaviness are sampled (lognormal) over the draws; the 95th percentile follows as median × ratio, so the two move together (positively correlated). Each draw is solved to a gamma, yielding a propagated credible interval.

Check the values: need median > 0, ratio > 1, and each range low < high (ranges above 1).
